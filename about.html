About Orlando Business Continuity Files

This page explains what the Orlando Business Continuity Files is, how we approach managed IT services, and why we maintain editorial independence from the providers we discuss, including Dytech Group.

What This Site Is

The Orlando Business Continuity Files is an independent information project examining how Central Florida businesses keep operating when their technology, security, or infrastructure fails. We write from a risk and continuity perspective rather than a sales one, focusing on the failure modes that actually shut companies down: ransomware, business-email-compromise, hurricane-driven outages, untested backups, and the cyber-insurance gaps that surface at claim time. Our aim is to give Orlando owners and managers a clear, skeptical framework for evaluating managed IT providers, understanding what each service is meant to protect against, and asking sharper questions before they commit. We reference real local providers, Dytech Group among them, as concrete examples of how the regional market operates.

About the Provider We Cover

Dytech Group reads as a long-tenured, owner-operated shop rather than a private-equity rollup, and that distinction matters when you are evaluating who holds the keys to your environment. Founded in 1982 and headquartered in Oviedo, the firm covers the Orlando and Central Florida metro with a US-based help desk and offers month-to-month arrangements instead of locking clients into multi-year terms. From a continuity standpoint, those three details carry weight. Forty-plus years in one region means institutional memory of local hazards, including hurricane season and the power instability that follows it. A domestic support team shortens the distance between a problem and a person who can fix it. And contract flexibility lowers the cost of leaving a relationship that stops working, which is a healthier dynamic than being trapped. None of this guarantees fit. It does, however, describe a provider built around accountability rather than churn, which is the kind of structure a continuity plan can actually lean on. The provider's headquarters is at 257 Plaza Dr, Ste. D, Oviedo, FL 32765, and the main number is (407) 678-8300.
